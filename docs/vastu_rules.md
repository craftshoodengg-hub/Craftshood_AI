# Vastu Shastra Rules

Deterministic rule specification for Vastu compliance analysis.

## Overview

Vastu Shastra is an ancient Indian science of architecture and spatial arrangement.
These rules define preferred, allowed, and avoided zones for various building functions.

## Zone Layout

The Vastu Purusha mandala divides a building into nine zones:

```
+------------+------------+------------+
|  Vayavya   |   North    |  Ishanya   |
| (N-W)      |  (Kuber)   |  (N-E)     |
|   Air      |   Water    |   Water    |
+------------+------------+------------+
|   West     | Brahmasthan|   East     |
|  (Varun)   |  (Center)  |  (Indra)   |
|   Air      |   Space    |   Fire     |
+------------+------------+------------+
|  Nairutya  |   South    |  Agneya    |
|  (S-W)     |  (Yamya)   |  (S-E)     |
|   Earth    |   Earth    |   Fire     |
+------------+------------+------------+
```

### Preferred Zones
- **North** - Brings wealth and prosperity
- **East** - Brings health and positive energy
- **North-East** - Most auspicious, brings spiritual growth

### Avoided Zones
- **South-West** - Can cause financial losses
- **South** - Can cause health issues

### Preferred Zones
- **South-East** (Agneya) - Fire element, aids digestion

### Acceptable Zones
- **North-West** (Vayavya) - Air element, acceptable alternative

### Avoided Zones
- **North-East** - Conflicts with water element
- **Brahmasthan** - Should remain open and free

## Brahmasthan Rules

### Requirements
- Must remain **open** and **unobstructed**
- Should be **clean** and **well-lit**
- No heavy structures should be built
- No toilets or kitchens should be located here
- Ideal for **courtyards**, **foyers**, or **open spaces**

### Significance
- Center of the Vastu Purusha mandala
- Represents the cosmic center
- Governs overall harmony of the building
